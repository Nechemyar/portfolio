Questions

ASK.

Why not WordPress or a template?

Templates look like the next 10,000 sites using the same one, which makes you forgettable when someone is comparing five tabs. WordPress can work, but for focused service sites it adds plugin bloat, security chores, and maintenance costs that shouldn't exist.

How long will my site take?

Most projects ship in 2–4 weeks from kick-off. Bigger scopes with CMS, multilingual pages, or integrations can take 4–6. You get the timeline in the proposal before anything starts.

What about hosting and maintenance?

Hosting on Vercel or Netlify runs free or a few dollars a month. I set it up under your account so you own it. Maintenance is optional — most clients only need occasional changes after launch.

Can I edit content myself?

Yes. Growth includes a lightweight CMS so you can edit copy, swap images, and add posts without touching code. Essential suits sites that stay fairly stable.

Do I own the code?

Completely. The full source goes to your GitHub at handoff. Take it to any developer, move hosts, do whatever you want with it.

What if I need changes after launch?

Growth includes a month of post-launch tweaks. After that, changes are hourly or a small retainer. Either way, you'll get a rate before I start.
